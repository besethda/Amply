Artist Listener

What's gone wrong with streaming?

If you’re a working musician, you know how tough it is to earn from your art.
Spotify, Apple Music, SoundCloud — they’re great for exposure, but terrible for income. Most artists earn fractions of a cent per stream. Unless you’re Bruno Mars, streaming alone won’t pay your bills.

...Introducing Amply

Amply isn’t another streaming service.
It’s a hosting and payment platform that lets artists decide how much their streams are worth. You upload your music, set your per-stream price, and Amply automatically charges listeners based on their play activity. You keep full control and full profit.

How does it work??

Spotify pays out about 70% of its revenue on artist royalties, yet most artists get almost nothing. Amply changes that by not having royalties. We simply host your music using the same cloud infrastructure (AWS) that powers Spotify, giving you high-quality global streaming at a fraction of the cost. Listeners pay directly for what they hear, not for bloated subscription plans.
The average listener spends about 114 minutes a day on music — roughly 3,500 streams per month. At $0.004 per stream, that’s around $4.50, less than half of a typical $12 Spotify subscription. With Amply, artists earn more, and listeners pay less.

How is Amply different from a streaming platform??

Good question! A streaming platform hosts copyrighted content and distributes it. Amply does not host anything itself, it merely acts as an interface for artists to use their own hosting and streaming services. Amply also does not manage payments, they go directly between the user and artist. When it comes to royalties, Amply does not have royalties since artists control their content prices and distribution.
